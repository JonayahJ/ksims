The Research

Recovery from Mobility Limitation in Middle-Aged African Americans

Takeaways

Compared to their White counterparts, African Americans have higher rates of mobility difficulty, which impairs functioning in daily life and leads to reduced independence. Most evidence on mobility limitations focuses on risk factors, such as cardiovascular disease, which African Americans are also disproportionately diagnosed with. Loss in functioning is a dynamic process that begins in middle age. Accordingly, we evaluated disability risk among aging adults by evaluating factors associated with recovery.

(1) There was a high rate of incident functional limitation in walking or taking stairs.

(2) A high proportion of participants recovered from these limitations within one year.

(3) There were no significant associations between body size measures or exercise frequency and recovery.

(4) However, low income, experiencing more frequent discrimination in daily life, and having more comorbidities such as cancer or heart failure were associated with a lower likelihood of recovery.
