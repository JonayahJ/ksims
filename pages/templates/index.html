About Me

Dr. Kendra D. Sims, PhD

With a background in epidemiology, biostatistics, and computer science, my research identifies modifiable population-level risk factors for poor health outcomes. My ultimate goal is to inform policy and translational research that optimizes health over the life course, particularly for marginalized groups.

Health is a human right, systematically denied to large groups of people. The stress from this inequality has manifested as stark disparities in preventable disability and premature mortality. Across projects, I quantify the importance of positive environment, social supports, as well as mental health hygiene.

Why This Work Matters

More rigorous research, unpacking the complicated chain linking societal stressors to subsequent poor health, is needed to resolve the stark disparities in later life health. I emphasize cultural competency in messages tailored for policy-makers, health care providers, as well as the average citizen. I believe health researchers who represent the populations most at risk are well-suited to convey such messages. The ultimate goal of my work is advancing strategies that address health disparities.

“I contribute to the growing body of evidence that quantifies the many poor health outcomes downstream from biased treatment. To that end, data science is my main outlet for advocacy.”

Dr. Kendra D. Sims, PhD
