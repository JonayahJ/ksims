About Me

Who Am I

Dr. Kendra D. Sims, PhD

Instead of granting all residents equal opportunity to age with dignity, many Americans experience chronic conditions that lead to high healthcare costs and reduced quality of life. I contribute to the growing body of evidence that quantifies the poor health outcomes downstream from inequality on the individual as well as structural level. To that end, data science is my outlet for advocacy.

By focusing on factors such as race and socioeconomic status as independent risk factors for disease, the medical model treats marginalized people as doomed to premature morbidity and mortality. In my research, I push back against the assumption that sociodemographic factors are innate and unchangeable determinants of health. Instead, political and social institutions have perpetuated generations of health inequity. I ultimately want to develop translational interventions and policies to help older adults prevent, delay, and recover from disabling conditions.

The importance of a lifelong investment in health becomes more evident as we approach older age. I want to provide people resources to age independently in their communities. These health-promoting tools include knowledge about and opportunities for sustainable health behaviors, fostering social connections, and managing ambient stress. To achieve health equity, we will need clinicians and policy makers to facilitate changes in the social and built environment.

“I contribute to the growing body of evidence that quantifies the many poor health outcomes downstream from biased treatment. To that end, data science is my main outlet for advocacy.”

Dr. Kendra D. Sims, PhD
