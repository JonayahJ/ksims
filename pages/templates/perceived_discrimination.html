The Research

Perceived Discrimination and Trajectory of High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein among African Americans

Takeaways

Stressors such as discrimination are thought to active the physiological stress response. We evaluated associations between perceived unequal treatment in daily life as well as from societal institutions and changes over time in high sensitivity C-reactive protein (hs-CRP).

(1) Participants reporting more frequent everyday discrimination and more experiences of lifetime discrimination had modestly greater average 13-year increases in hs-CRP.

(2) Body size did not mediate associations between discrimination measures and hs-CRP trajectories.

(3) Men reporting higher everyday or lifetime discrimination had lower levels of hs-CRP at baseline.

Citation

Sims KD, Sims M, Glover L, Smit E, Odden MC. Perceived Discrimination and Trajectories of C-Reactive Protein: The Jackson Heart Study. American Journal of Preventative Medicine. February 2020.
